The robed griffon watched the sky for hours that felt at once eternal and all too brief, wondering if the night was cold, unable to remember what cold felt like but staying there nonetheless, silent, with the same quiet determination that had kept him alive all these centuries.

He lifted a claw, and what came from under his robes was bone, bereft of even sinew. And thought he kept it outstretched for the longest time, nothing happened.
